Research

Thesis Title: “Real-Time Obscene Scene Nudity Detection and Blurring in a Video Clip”

This research demonstrates a real-time neural network-based system to detect and blur nude and obscene content in videos, enhancing online safety for children and sensitive viewers. Using YOLOv6 and OpenCV, the system achieves a balance of accuracy and speed suitable for live video streams.

Problem & Motivation

Nudity and sexual content are widely accessible online, posing risks to children and vulnerable users. Existing detection algorithms struggle with real-time performance and false positives, motivating the development of a fast and accurate content filtering system.

Methodology

  • Dataset: 3,500 labeled images split into training (2,450), validation (700), and test (350) sets
  • Algorithms: YOLOv5, YOLOv6, Faster R-CNN (Detectron2)
  • Blurring: OpenCV average blur identified as fastest and most effective method
  • Evaluation: mAP@0.5, precision-recall curves, and blur speed benchmarking

Results & Insights

YOLOv6 achieved faster detection than YOLOv5 with comparable accuracy. OpenCV average blur provided real-time concealment with minimal latency. This combination ensures safe video content delivery without affecting normal viewing experience.

Impact & Contribution

This research contributes to real-time obscene content filtering, demonstrating advanced skills in neural networks, computer vision, and image processing. It also highlights expertise in dataset creation, performance evaluation, and practical deployment of deep learning models.

My Contribution

While this thesis was a collaborative effort, my primary contributions included designing and implementing the real-time detection pipeline, fine-tuning the YOLOv6 model for optimal performance, and evaluating multiple blurring techniques to identify the fastest and most reliable method for concealing obscene content. I was responsible for dataset preparation, labeling, and preprocessing to ensure high-quality training data. Additionally, I documented all experimental results, analyzed model performance metrics, and proposed improvements to reduce false positives, thereby enhancing the practical applicability of the system. Through this work, I gained hands-on experience in neural networks, computer vision, and real-time video processing, which strengthened both my technical and research skills.

Future Work

Future work includes expanding the dataset, improving detection of small-scale obscene regions, and integrating the system into live streaming platforms for broader deployment.
